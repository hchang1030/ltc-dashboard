3. The 4-Step Interactive Order Selection:
   Inside the expanded container, display a vertical stack of the 4 procedural steps with interactive checkboxes mapped to local state.

   - STEP 1: Diagnostic Workup & Allied Health Consults
     [ ] Baseline Diagnostics: Order CBC, Albumin, TSH, Electrolytes, Creatinine, Calcium, and Fasting Glucose.
     [ ] Dentition Check: Inspect mouth for poorly fitting dentures, severe dental caries, dry mouth (xerostomia), or oral candidiasis (thrush).
     [ ] Depression Screening: Administer the Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) or PHQ-9 modified for dementia.
     [ ] Consult Dietitian: Comprehensive nutritional assessment, calorie count, and food preference audit.
     [ ] Consult Speech-Language Pathology (SLP): Swallowing evaluation if signs of dysphagia (coughing/choking during meals) are present.

   - STEP 2: Medication Review & Deprescribing
     [ ] Audit Medication Regimen: Review and taper/discontinue appetite-suppressing medications if clinically safe: Cholinesterase Inhibitors (donepezil, galantamine, rivastigmine), Activating SSRIs (fluoxetine), Topiramate, and chronic Digoxin (rule out toxicity).
     [ ] Supplement Consolidation: Consolidate or eliminate non-essential supplements to address high-pill-burden configurations.

   - STEP 3: Dietary & Nursing Interventions
     [ ] Weight & Fluid Monitoring: Implement weekly weights x 4 weeks, then monthly once stable. Record food and fluid intake records x 7 days.
     [ ] Food Enhancement: Provide nutrient-dense options (e.g., add real butter, cream, or gravy to meals; offer full-fat dairy/whole milk instead of skim).
     [ ] Dining Environment: Provide feeding assistance or verbal cueing during meals. Serve meals in a calm, socially engaging dining environment. Encourage small, frequent finger foods and snacks between meals.

   - STEP 4: Pharmacological Appetite Stimulation (Safe Alternatives)
     - Display an inline info tag: "Criteria: Consider only if non-pharmacological interventions fail, weight loss is severe (>5% in 1 month or >10% in 6 months), and it aligns with Goals of Care."
